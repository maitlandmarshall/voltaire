# The Trial of Shar

#lore #shar #trial

![Shar’s trial motif](./Trial_of_Shar_motif.png)

## Summary

A structured “trial” framework associated with [[Shar]] and Voltaire’s proposed game/bet, intended to test Voltaire (and potentially others) through symbolic, metaphysical challenges.

## What’s known

- Shar accepted the premise of a game/trial but declined to participate as a player (per existing notes).
- The trial has been associated with a symbolic framework (circle/square/triangle) in prior lore.
- **[Voltaire-only] (planned framing)** Voltaire proposed hosting the trial inside [[Head-Space]] using his rulebook artifact [[Machinations & Actions - Player's Handbook]].

## Palashaey Map — Shape Echoes (Voltaire-only | Inferred)

Using `Codex/Places/Palashaey_map_original.png` as a reference (handout image labeled “Palischuk,” which is the same city as Palashaey at this table), the city’s layout contains several prominent, repeated shapes that *could* echo Shar’s circle/square/triangle framework:

### Circles

- **Public Dias**: a large, explicit circular civic space.
- A **large concentric-ring circle** near the **Tower of the Warlock Knights** (reads like a ritual/scrying diagram).
- **Official Arena**: another explicit circle.

### Squares / Rectangles

- The dense **grid block** district (west/central) reads as “square/order/administration.”
- Multiple **rectangular compounds** (Orders/Foundry/Distillery/Granaries) reinforce the “planned” geometry.

### Triangles / Angular wedges

- Several districts are divided into **triangular pasture/farm partitions** in the south.
- The **Temple of Blood** is an octagon-like frame with **triangular motifs** inside (reads like “triangle” symbolism embedded into a larger seal).

### Working hypothesis (useful in play)

- If Shar’s trial cares about *symbolic movement*, a plausible “shape route” in this city would be:
  - **Circle** → Public Dias (or the concentric-ring node),
  - **Square** → grid/administrative/industrial blocks,
  - **Triangle** → Temple of Blood / triangular farmland partitions.
