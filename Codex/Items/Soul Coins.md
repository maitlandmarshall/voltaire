# Soul Coins

![Import motif](./Imports_motif.png)

## Summary

Infernal currency used as stakes and/or ritual components in Voltaire’s notes, notably tied to demon-gate work that summoned [[Glasya]].

## What the Party Knows (in-play)

- Voltaire made a high-stakes bet with a horned demon for **16 soul coins** in the lead-up to completing the demon-gate ritual (**[To verify]** exact demon identity).
- After the ritual succeeded, the demon refused to honor the deal and Voltaire destroyed it with [[Sun Card]] (source: `Adventures/Voltaire's Notes/Voltaire's D&D Notes.json`).

## Voltaire-Only Notes

- Voltaire reports the soul coins were **imbued into the [[Crab Book]]**, where they now appear as **sketches of coins** rather than “normal” stored objects.
- Paper notes mention “16 soul coins” in the context of [[Demon Milk]] (**[To verify]** whether this is a yield, cost, or a separate stake).
