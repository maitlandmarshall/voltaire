## Summary

Voltaire can “smell” books and sense the author’s first-person experience at the time of writing: a synesthetic imprint that carries emotion, sensation, and intent.

## What it does (as described)

- Smell a book and perceive sensory/experiential echoes from the author’s perspective.
- Treats books as living artifacts with a readable “scent signature.”

## Uses

- Identify authorship or provenance by “scent.”
- Detect emotional truth vs. performative writing (speculative; depends on table rulings).
