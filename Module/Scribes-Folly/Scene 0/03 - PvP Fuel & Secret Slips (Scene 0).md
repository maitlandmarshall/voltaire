# PvP Fuel & Secret Slips (Scene 0)

Use these to push PvP without hard-forcing it. The key is **public rewards** + **private pressure**.

## Public PvP Fuel (announce out loud)

- **Scoreboard reward:** “Top damage dealer this round earns a Key-Tag.”
- **First blood reward:** “First blood earns +1 Favor.”
- **Crown of Ink:** at end of Round 1, the creature who dealt the most damage gains a visible crown; the crown-holder gets +1 Favor immediately.
  - The crown can be stolen by reducing the holder to 0 HP (nonlethal is normal in Head-Space™), or by Sleight of Hand vs Perception (action).

## Private Slips (hand these out secretly)

Cut/paste and customize names. Each slip should offer a concrete benefit that nudges conflict, plus a loophole that rewards clever “team betrayal” solutions.

### Generic templates (fill in PC names)

**Slip A — Glasya Clause**
> “I can draft you an exit clause. Deliver a Key-Tag to my Advocate. Do not let the others know. Payment due later.”

**Slip B — Shar’s Taste**
> “Make a loss. Not a death. A loss. Take something meaningful from an ally (or make them surrender it). You will be remembered.”

**Slip C — Beshaba’s Dare**
> “Be the first to pull the NEW RULE lever. If you survive the consequences, you gain a luck boon. If you fail, it’s hilarious.”

**Slip D — Protagonist Offer**
> “End this scene wearing the Crown of Ink and you keep one extra item through capture.”

**Slip E — The Quiet Key**
> “Steal a Key-Tag without being seen. If nobody accuses you out loud, you start the Ludus with +2 Favor.”

### In-canon examples (optional)

**Cornholio-flavored**
> “Shar watches. Prove you can choose *loss* without cruelty. Make someone give up an item willingly.”

**Dagoth-flavored**
> “Rules are real here. Find the lever. Pull it. Speak the rule before you roll. Your data will be… appreciated.”

**Cromash-flavored**
> “A donation is due. ‘Send’ one thing that Shar hates (not a person). You’ll be rewarded with a Key-Tag.”

## Session Slips (Current Roster)

These are written for tonight’s roster: **Vlad (Lachlan)**, **Kithari Razor-Ash (Jakabo)**, **Cornholio**, **Cromash**, **Dagoth**, **Yennefer**, and **(optional) Elora**.

Hand out **one slip each** at the start of Scene 0. Optionally hand out a second wave at the end of Round 2 if PvP needs a push.

### Vlad (Lachlan) — Human, CN, Fighter/Rogue, Outlander (last of tribe)

**Slip — Apex Legend**
> “The strong thrive. End Scene 0 wearing the Crown of Ink. If you do, you keep **one extra item** through capture.”

**Slip — Pack Tactics (Loophole)**
> “A lone wolf dies. A pack becomes legend. Choose an ally as your ‘packmate’. If they willingly give you an item (even a mundane token) before capture, you both start the Ludus with **+1 Favor**.”

**Slip — The Roll Call of Ash (Tribe Oath)**
> “You are the last. But the last can still be *loud*.”
>
> **Track this during Scene 0:** count the number of **distinct enemies** Vlad damages at least once (any damage source counts; an enemy is “counted” the first time you damage it).
>
> **If your count is 4+:** choose one name from your lost tribe. That person is **revived as an ally-echo** in the Ludus (Scene 1) and will fight beside you for one bout (your choice: Bout 1 or Bout 2).  
> *Ally-echo suggestion:* use **Veteran** or **Scout** stats; describe them as ink-and-bone, carrying a weapon your tribe was known for.
>
> **If your count is less than 4:** for each point below 4, choose **one tribe member** and one detail about them (name, face, voice, deed, or a private moment). That memory is **redacted**: you cannot speak it, write it, or recall it clearly for the rest of the one-shot. (Shar approves. Voltaire takes notes.)

### Kithari Razor-Ash (Jakabo) — Aarakocra Monk (Drunken Master), Rune Carver (Akadi), quest for the Galeheart Seal

**Slip — The Wind Points**
> “The winds carry a rune: **JOURNEY**. Pull the NEW RULE lever. If the result is harmful, laugh anyway. If you survive to capture, gain **a Key-Tag**.”

**Slip — Redirect the Draft**
> “A missed strike is a message. Before capture, use your chaos to turn an attack aside—make an enemy’s blow hit someone else (or try). If you do, the winds grant **advantage on one saving throw** in Scene 1.”

### Cornholio — Consort of Shar (Shadow’s Weave)

**Slip — Shar’s Standard**
> “Demonstrate loss without cruelty. Make someone surrender an item *willingly* (even temporarily). If you succeed, Shar marks you: start the Ludus with **+2 Favor**.”

**Slip — Jealousy Is Data**
> “End Scene 0 with the Crown of Ink, but do it **without dealing the most damage**. If you can, you keep **one extra item** through capture. Shar enjoys subtlety.”

### Cromash — Paladin of Grûumsh, Shar’s accidental celebrity (“donations”)

**Slip — The Donation**
> “Send Shar something she hates. During Scene 0, throw a valuable object into the Margin (it is *gone*). If you do, receive **a Key-Tag** in the smash cut.”

**Slip — Grûumsh’s Straight Line**
> “Prove your strength is not borrowed. Challenge an ally to a **nonlethal** duel for the Crown (first blood or first prone). If you win, gain **+1 Favor**; if you lose, you still gain **a Gate clue**.”

### Dagoth — Warlock chronicler, rules-arbitrator

**Slip — Record the Rule**
> “Speak the rule aloud before you roll. If you do this **three times** in Scene 0, Voltaire gives you a private note: **one Circle/Square/Triangle solution hint**.”

**Slip — Margin Audit (PvP bait)**
> “Catch someone cheating the scene. If you publicly accuse an ally of ‘breaking the rules’ (true or not) and the table argues about it for 10 seconds, gain **+1 Favor**. Voltaire loves proceedings.”

### Yennefer — Scorned-by-Shar wildcard, wings, whip

**Slip — Prove You’re the Protagonist**
> “Take the Crown of Ink from someone who already has it. If you end Scene 0 crowned, you start the Ludus with **a thrown satchel** (potion or Torn Page) landing at your feet.”

**Slip — Spite Letter (Loophole)**
> “Shar’s perfume still lingers. If you humiliate a rival *without* dealing damage (trip them, disarm them, steal from them, expose a secret), you gain **+2 Favor**. Nonlethal. Performative. Petty. Perfect.”

### Elora (optional) — Archer, prophetic receptiveness

**Slip — The Moon Poem Returns**
> “Stand in a Spotlight tile at the start of your turn and do not move until the end of that turn. If you do, you receive a vision: take **a Gate clue** and **+1 Favor** (but everyone sees you glowing).”
