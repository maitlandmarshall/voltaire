# Kithari Razor-Ash (Jakabo) — Session Slips (Scene 0)

Hand this player **one** slip at Scene 0 start. If PvP is slow, hand a second slip at end of Round 2.

## Slip — The Wind Points

> “The winds carry a rune: **JOURNEY**. Pull the NEW RULE lever. If the result is harmful, laugh anyway. If you survive to capture, gain **a Key-Tag**.”

## Slip — Redirect the Draft

> “A missed strike is a message. Before capture, use your chaos to turn an attack aside—make an enemy’s blow hit someone else (or try). If you do, the winds grant **advantage on one saving throw** in Scene 1.”

## Slip — Runes in the Haze (Galeheart Whisper)

> “The runes speak more clearly when you drink. Unfortunately, so does the void.”
>
> **Once per round in Scene 0**, you may take a dramatic “swig” (purely flavor) before you roll an attack, save, or ability check. When you do, choose one:
> - **Clarity:** add **1d4** to that roll (after rolling, before the result is declared).
> - **Wind-Read:** ask the DM one yes/no question about the scene (*‘Is the lever trapped?’ ‘Is that crown bait?’ ‘Is someone lying?’*). The DM answers truthfully, but vaguely.
>
> Each time you do this, you also carve that moment into a rune-stone in your pocket. The DM writes down a 5–10 word “rune memory” (e.g., *“I laughed as the rules broke”*).
>
> **At the end of Scene 0:**
> - If you have **3+ rune memories**, choose one and declare it **True**. You gain a **Galeheart Echo** ally for Scene 1 (a small wind-spirit that can, once, either: shove a creature 10 ft, grant you advantage on one roll, or carry a Key-Tag 30 ft to you).
> - If you have **fewer than 3 rune memories**, one of your runes goes blank. Choose: you lose access to either **DEATH** or **JOURNEY** (your Rune Shaper spell) for the rest of the one-shot, *or* you voluntarily give up a personal memory about the Skybreaker Monastery (you can’t speak it or write it).
