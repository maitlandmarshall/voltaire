# Monsters (Scene 0)

These are tuned for a cinematic “unwinnable” set-piece. The boss is designed to *feel* fightable while being mathematically immune to nova via damage caps + regen + scripted capture.

## The Redactor of the Draft
*Huge construct (aberrant), unaligned*

**Armor Class** 19 (ink-plate)  
**Hit Points** 420 (the number is a prop; see Ink Regeneration + Damage Cap)  
**Speed** 30 ft., fly 30 ft. (hover)

STR 22 (+6) DEX 14 (+2) CON 22 (+6) INT 10 (+0) WIS 16 (+3) CHA 18 (+4)

**Saving Throws** Dex +7, Con +11, Wis +8, Cha +9  
**Skills** Perception +8  
**Damage Resistances** cold, fire, lightning; bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical attacks  
**Damage Immunities** necrotic, psychic (optional: make this resistance if your table hates it)  
**Condition Immunities** charmed, frightened, exhausted, grappled, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned, prone, restrained  
**Senses** blindsight 30 ft., darkvision 120 ft., passive Perception 18  
**Languages** understands all; doesn’t speak (Voltaire narrates)  
**Challenge** Not for XP (set-piece)

### Traits

**Damage Cap.** The Redactor takes no more than **35 damage from a single source** (one hit, spell, feature, or effect). Excess damage is ignored.

**Ink Regeneration.** At the start of its turn, the Redactor regains **30 hit points** if it has at least 1 hit point.

**Legendary Resistance (3/Day).** If the Redactor fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.

**Unperson (Axiom of the Draft).** A creature reduced to 0 HP by the Redactor is **stable** (unconscious) instead of dying. Voltaire wants data, not corpses.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The Redactor makes two attacks: one with its Quill Scythe and one with its Margin Lash.

**Quill Scythe.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. *Hit:* 2d10 + 6 slashing damage plus 2d8 psychic damage.

**Margin Lash.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 30 ft., one target. *Hit:* 2d8 + 6 bludgeoning damage, and the target must succeed on a DC 17 Strength saving throw or be pulled up to 20 feet toward the Redactor and knocked prone.

**Redaction Wave (Recharge 5–6).** The Redactor exhales a cone of erasing ink in a 60-foot cone. Each creature in that area must make a DC 17 Constitution saving throw, taking 6d8 necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a success. On a failure, the creature can’t take reactions until the start of its next turn.

### Reactions

**Footnote Parry (1/round).** The Redactor reduces the damage it would take from one source by 25.

### Legendary Actions

The Redactor can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature’s turn.

**Edit Step.** The Redactor moves up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks.

**Line Break.** One creature the Redactor can see within 60 feet must succeed on a DC 17 Dexterity saving throw or be restrained by binding straps until the end of its next turn (escape DC 17 as an action).

**Insert Clause (Costs 2 Actions).** The Redactor targets one creature it can see within 60 feet. Until the end of that creature’s next turn, the first time it hits with an attack or spell, it also hits the nearest ally within 10 feet for half the damage dealt (minimum 1). If no ally is within 10 feet, the clause fizzles.

## Editorial Enforcer
*Medium construct, unaligned*

Use **Guard** statistics, with these changes:

- Type becomes **construct**.
- Immune to poison and exhaustion; doesn’t need to breathe.
- Weapon becomes **Quill Spear**: melee, 1d6 + 1 piercing + 1d4 psychic (it “edits” your confidence).
- Trait: **Narrative Compliance.** If a creature attempts to leave the “scene boundary” (see environment file), the Enforcer immediately uses its reaction to move up to 15 ft. and make one Quill Spear attack.

## Aggressive Glyph Swarm
*Medium swarm of Tiny constructs, unaligned*

Use **Swarm of Bats** with these changes:

- Creature type becomes **construct**.
- Damage becomes **slashing** (razor letters).
- Condition immunities include frightened, poisoned.
- Flavor: the swarm is a cloud of punctuation marks and glowing serif letters.
