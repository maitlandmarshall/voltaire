# The Scribe’s Folly
## A gladiatorial escape one-shot inside [Voltaire](<../../Codex/Characters/Party/Voltaire.md>)’s Head-Space™

### What this is

This one-shot begins **directly after** `Adventures/2025-11-22.md`. The temple, the desert above it, and the campaign itself start to feel like *draft text*—as if reality has always been an annotated page inside [Voltaire](<../../Codex/Characters/Party/Voltaire.md>)’s skull.

It opens with a massive, cinematic, **unwinnable** set-piece fight. The point isn’t “win or die”; the point is what the party manages to **protect, steal, learn, or prove** before Voltaire *captures them* for his arena.

From there they awaken in a “ludus” made of bookbinding, crab shell, and rules-lawyer logic. They must survive bouts that pit them against **each other** and the **environment**, navigate cage-time intrigue with rival gladiators and sponsors, and resolve a Shar-flavored “trial” that ends with a choice: **escape**… or become **the Protagonist**.

### Themes

- Psychological horror (loss, memory, identity)
- Spartacus-style captivity (rivals, patrons, freedom-for-a-price)
- Meta-narrative weird (rules as physics; “new rule!” announcements)
- Dualism (vile/exalted knowledge inside Voltaire; light vs dark)

### Safety / table-fit (recommend saying out loud)

- Captivity themes; body-horror imagery (ink-as-blood, living book); mind-control vibe (unreliable rules).
- Offer an “off-ramp” (fade-to-black, swap imagery, or make it a “dream-trial” with no lasting trauma).

# DM Quickstart

### Recommended party

- Works best for **Level 8–12** PCs (or scale using the guidance below).
- Assumes the PCs are the campaign party and starts from the last known state of `Adventures/2025-11-22.md`.

### Scene 0 pack (unwinnable fight)

- Prep + script: [Scene 0 — Reality Slip & Capture](<Scene 0/00 - Scene 0 - Reality Slip & Capture.md>)
- Monsters: [Scene 0 — Monsters](<Scene 0/01 - Monsters (Scene 0).md>)
- Environment + interactables/tables: [Scene 0 — Environment](<Scene 0/02 - Environment (Scene 0).md>)
- PvP fuel + secret slips: [Scene 0 — PvP Fuel & Slips](<Scene 0/03 - PvP Fuel & Secret Slips (Scene 0).md>)

### Runtime target (6 beats)

If you want this to fit in a single session, **timebox hard**:

1. **Prologue (temple reality-slip)** (10–15 min)
2. **Unwinnable fight → capture** (30–45 min)
3. **Cage-time & ludus choices** (30–45 min)
4. **Bout 1 (environment + pressure)** (45–60 min)
5. **Intermission (cages, deals, gate puzzle)** (30–40 min)
6. **Bout 2 + finale (PvP dial + escape)** (60–75 min)

### Core loop

- Between bouts: social pressure + puzzle clues + sabotage options.
- In the arena: spectacle + weird rule twists + a clear objective.

### Voltaire on the Throne (run him as the antagonist, not a stat block)

Voltaire is *present*. He sits on a king’s throne above the arena (or above the temple, once it “becomes” the arena), writing in his crab-book and **making up rules** mid-fight.

Use him like a lair:
- He doesn’t need hit points; he needs **screen time** and **authority**.
- Rule changes should create *new plays*, not shut players down. If a rule “nerfs”, it should also create a *clever loophole* the party can exploit.
- When you want to signal “this is his mind”, have Voltaire say it plainly: *“Of course you’re real. I wrote you.”*
- If a PC insists “this can’t be real”, have Voltaire react like they’re arguing with gravity: *“Outside? There is no outside.”*

### PvP dial (make it optional and safe)

The arena rewards betrayal, but it should never hard-force it.
- Use **incentives**, not compulsion: extra gear, Favor, sponsor attention, “protagonist” perks.
- Allow **nonlethal** takedowns and easy reconciliation.
- If your table doesn’t enjoy PvP, run Bout 2 as “co-op vs illusionary doppelgangers” and keep the rivalry purely social.

### Audience Favor (simple meter)

Track **Favor (0–6)**. Start at **1**.

Gain +1 when a PC:
- Does something dramatic, clever, or thematic (speech, mercy, showmanship, “rules-lawyer” win).
- Turns a Voltaire “new rule” against the arena in an entertaining way.

Lose -1 when the party:
- Stalls, refuses to engage, or tries to “leave the module” with zero theatrics.

Spend Favor (once per bout, unless noted):
- **2 Favor:** A thrown satchel lands in the sand: 1 potion of healing *or* 1 “Torn Page” (DM picks a 1st-level spell scroll effect).
- **4 Favor:** A sponsor intervenes: remove one arena hazard *or* grant the party a short rest as a montage (no time passes).
- **6 Favor (once total):** “The Crowd Demands a Twist.” The party chooses one: reinforcements arrive for the enemy *or* the enemy is swapped for a weirder, funnier, scarier one (your pick, but let them feel the leverage).

# Prologue — Where We Left Off (2025-11-22)

Start in the temple exactly where the session ended:

- The party is at the stairs / lower level threshold.
- The ancient druid (Aelrindë) has revealed herself after masquerading as Voltaire.
- The last words hang in the air: **“Do not fear, you will not break, you will bend.”**

Then blur the line, fast:

- The **dark mythallar** hums like a throat clearing before a speech.
- The pillars and walls “gain headings” (your notes start appearing as if they are page titles: *## Page 1*, *## Page 2*).
- In torchlight, you can see faint file-name-glyphs and “page numbers” crawling over stone like ants.
- A whisper that isn’t Voltaire’s (Shar) brushes the edge of hearing: *“The Scribe awaits.”*
- And then Voltaire’s voice, delighted and offended at the idea reality could be anything but his: *“Ah. There you are. Finally.”*

Do **not** cut to black yet. Go straight into the set piece.

# Scene 0 — The Unwinnable Fight: The King Takes the Board

This is the “bang”. It must feel like a boss fight… but it’s secretly a **scorecard** for what they carry into the arena.

### The reveal

The room “unfolds” like a pop-up book. A **king’s throne** rises from the dark mythallar as if it’s always been there.

Voltaire sits on it—unblinking smile, crab-book-tail draped like a royal train—watching the party like miniatures on a battle map.

> “Welcome to my campaign.”

### Run it as a doomed 4-round combat

Tell the players up front (in-world, via Voltaire) what success looks like:

> “You cannot win this. You can only impress me.”

- Roll initiative and play 4 rounds.
- On initiative count 20 each round, Voltaire triggers a **Lair Rule** (below).
- At the end of Round 4, Voltaire **Redacts** the scene and captures the party.

### Enemies (don’t overprep)

Use whatever is already “in the room” from `Adventures/2025-11-22.md`, but re-skinned as paper/ink manifestations:

- The creeping god-golems return as **Papercraft Colossi**.
- The druid’s “thousand masks” becomes a shifting **chorus of faces** in the shadows.
- Anything the party tries to destroy “reprints” with an errata note.

The specifics don’t matter because the fight is unwinnable; the *moments* matter.

### Voltaire’s Lair Rules (pick 1 each round)

1. **New Rule: Gravity is Optional.** Each creature chooses to jump absurdly far or be knocked prone by invisible “footnotes”.
2. **New Rule: First Blood Owns the Spotlight.** The first creature to deal damage this round gains +1 Favor (yes, even if it’s a PC hitting a PC).
3. **New Rule: Redaction.** A 10-foot square becomes a black bar; creatures inside are blinded until they leave it.
4. **New Rule: Continuity Error.** One PC’s last spoken sentence becomes *true physics* for 6 seconds (one round). Interpret generously and weirdly.

### “Victory in defeat” (choose what they earn)

Each PC can attempt **one** of these on their turn (instead of attacking) once during the fight:

- **Smuggle** (Sleight of Hand or Arcana DC 15): keep **one extra item** beyond the “signature item” rule.
- **Scar the Page** (weapon damage or Arcana/Religion DC 16): tear a “page seam” in the room; later, the party gets a **spine-tunnel advantage** (bypass one arena hazard or skip one lock requirement).
- **Make a Rival** (Intimidation or Deception DC 15): mark a gladiator/NPC in the crowd; they will **target** the party later (but doing so gains +1 Favor now).
- **Make an Ally** (Persuasion or Medicine DC 15): save an NPC from being “edited out”; that NPC appears in the cages later and owes the party.

### The capture (end of Round 4)

Voltaire closes his book like a judge closing a case file.

Ink rises from the floor, becoming **words**—sentences wrapping around wrists, throats, weapons. The last thing they see is Voltaire leaning forward on his throne, smiling in pain and delight.

> “Now. Let’s workshop you.”

Cut to black.

# Scene 1 — The Cage of Commentary (Ludus of Margins)

The party wakes in an iron cage that is actually **book binding**: leather straps, brass corners, stitched seams. The bars are quills.

Their skin prickles: each has a fresh, faint tattoo at the base of the thumb—**a tiny glyph of circle, square, or triangle** (assign randomly or by vibe). It feels like a bookmark.

### Immediate stakes (tell them plainly)

- The gates are locked.
- The arena is above.
- “Freedom” is possible, but **the rules are not fair**.

### Gear (pick one approach)

**A) Keep signature gear (recommended for campaign PCs):**
- Each PC keeps **1 signature item** (weapon, focus, or armor) and loses the rest “to the lockers”.

**B) Arena loadout (clean for new PCs):**
- Everyone starts with simple gear (PHB baseline). First Favor spend can “drop” a better item.

### Key-Tags (lockers + the Vault)

Key-Tags are brass locker tags the Quillmaster uses as both **keys** and **currency**.

- **1 Key-Tag:** open 1 locker (recover 1 confiscated item).
- **Loose tags (arena loot):** tags can appear as scattered pickups. Grabbing one normally costs an action, but the arena may grant “free” grabs to anyone who spilled an ally’s blood that round.
- **Excess Key-Tags:** can be “bundled” in the Ludus to open the **Vault of Margins** (including legendary stage-prop gear usable in the one-shot). See [Vault of Margins (Key-Tag Rewards)](<Scene 0/04 - Vault of Margins (Key-Tag Rewards).md>).

### Scene 1 Add-On: True Polymorph as PvP Bait (the “Form Writ”)

If you want the table to *choose* PvP (instead of feeling forced into it), make the temptation concrete: give them access to a **True Polymorph-like effect** that is most “profitable” when used on another PC.

**Pitch (Quillmaster, smiling through stitches):**
> “A ninth-circle rewrite. One sentence. One signature. One *volunteer*… or one *winner*.”

**The item:** *Form Writ (Torn Page)* — a single-use, ink-stamped “scroll” that functions like **True Polymorph**, but is explicitly **mindscape-only**.

- **Use:** 1 action, 30 ft, requires line of sight. The user must **read the Writ aloud** (Voltaire hears it).
- **Targeting:** you can’t target yourself with it (this is the social wedge). Target is either:
  - **willing**, or
  - **incapacitated / restrained** (i.e., you “earned the signature”).
- **Save:** Wisdom save (suggest DC 18) to resist *unwilling* rewriting.
- **Forms:** keep it fun and table-fast:
  - creature → creature only (no objects),
  - no legendary / mythic creatures,
  - cap at roughly the target’s CR/level (or “big beast / scary humanoid / weird demon-looking thing” by vibe).
- **Duration:** concentration up to 10 minutes.
  - If maintained for the full duration, the form becomes “ink-stable” **until the end of the module** (it still snaps back when they exit Voltaire’s head).
- **Counterplay:** ending concentration ends the effect; **Dispel Magic** ends it (treat as 9th-level for the check); knocking the caster unconscious ends it.

**How to hand it out (pick one dial):**

- **Soft (recommend first run):** the sponsor offers **one Form Writ** in exchange for drama. If a PC uses it on **another PC**, gain **+2 Favor** and **+1 Key-Tag**. If they use it on an NPC/monster, gain **+1 Favor** only.
- **Medium:** the Quillmaster puts a sealed Form Writ on a hook outside the cage: “first nonlethal KO” gets it.
- **Hard (PvP-forward table):** the **Gate of Forms** in the Ludus only accepts a “Form signature.” The cleanest solution is willing cooperation, but the fastest solution is a nonlethal takedown and rewrite.

**Safety note to say out loud (10 seconds):**
- “This module offers PvP incentives and body-rewrite magic. Keep it **nonlethal**, keep it **fun**, and if anyone’s not into it we’ll pivot to co-op targets / cosmetic forms only.”

### Key NPCs (keep it small)

**The Lanista: The Quillmaster**
- A paper-pressed humanoid in a mask of stitched margins.
- Wants “good scenes”. Rewards drama and betrayal equally.

**Shar’s Raven Herald**
- Watches from a beam. It never speaks, but drops *clues* (see puzzles).

**A Sponsor (choose one)**
- **Glasya’s Advocate** (a polite devil with perfect posture): offers contracts, bailouts, and “legal exits”.
- **Beshaba’s Mascot** (a greasy little luck-imp): offers “help” that always costs something later.

**Rival gladiator (use an in-canon face)**
- **Sharias** (blue tiefling, Shar devotee): flirts with violence; offers “fun games”; needles Yennefer.
- If Sharias doesn’t fit your table, use a **Shadar-kai admirer** who recognizes Cromash’s “donations”.

### The cage scene (give them room to talk)

Before the first bout, let them sit in the cage and talk:

- With each other (planning / paranoia / “is this even real?”).
- With rivals in neighboring cages (alliances, sabotage, rumors).
- With the Quillmaster (terms, punishments, “good television”).

Seed the doubt here:
- A rival repeats a detail from an earlier campaign moment the party never said out loud.
- Someone swears they were in this cage **years ago** (but the party only arrived “a minute ago”).

### Other cages (quick roster)

Pick 2–3 faces so the scene has texture without becoming NPC soup:

- **Sharias** (blue tiefling; Shar devotee): “We can hurt each other, or we can hurt the rules. Both are fun.”
- **Shadar-kai admirer** (recognizes Cromash): asks for a “donation story” in exchange for a clue about the Gate.
- **Paper-bound veteran** (anonymous, scarred): claims Voltaire re-runs this arena to “revise” people; knows where the guards don’t look.

### Cage rumors (seed 1 true, 1 false, 1 half-true)

- “The Crown of Ink is a trap. Whoever wears it gets edited.”
- “The Gate of Forms opens if you bleed on the triangle, lie to the square, and forget the circle.”
- “If you make Voltaire laugh, he’ll loosen the bars. If you bore him, he’ll tighten the universe.”

### Ludus actions (give them choices)

Let the party pick **2** before Bout 1 (and **1** between later bouts):

1. **Make allies** (Persuasion/Insight DC 14): learn who hates who; gain 1 true rumor; optionally gain +1 Favor if you play it dramatically.
2. **Scout the locks** (Investigation/Thieves’ Tools DC 15): identify which of circle/square/triangle you’re missing and where to pay it.
3. **Train** (Athletics/Acrobatics DC 14): pick one PC; they gain advantage on their first grapple/shove/save vs restraint in Bout 1.
4. **Sabotage** (Stealth/Sleight of Hand DC 15): choose one Bout 1 hazard; it won’t trigger once.
5. **Perform** (Performance/Intimidation DC 14): gain +1 Favor before Bout 1 (and Voltaire heckles you either way).

# Scene 2 — Bout 1: Ink, Sand, and Bad Incentives

The arena is a book-spread the size of a stadium. The “sand” is shredded parchment. Every splash of blood becomes **ink** midair.

Above it all, Voltaire is visible on his throne, writing as he watches.

Voltaire (cheerful, clinical):
> “First impressions are everything. Let’s see how you handle unresolved material.”

### Encounter (baseline)

- 2× **Fae Echoes** (use *Specter* stats; damage becomes psychic; visuals are swamp-prince regret)
- 1× **Jilted Grudge** (use *Shadow* stats; visuals are a contract clause that learned to hate)

### Arena pressure (environment always matters)

At initiative count 20 each round, do **one**:
- **Ink surge:** ink floods a 15-foot line (Dex save DC 13 or knocked prone; terrain becomes difficult until next round).
- **Spine shift:** two “book spines” rise, creating cover and splitting the arena (forces repositioning).
- **Strap lash:** a binding strap grapples the creature with the highest current HP (escape DC 14).

### Twist (make it feel like Voltaire)

Also, at initiative count 20 each round, roll 1d4:
1. **Footnote:** One PC gains advantage on one roll *if they speak a “rule” aloud*.
2. **Redaction:** One enemy becomes resistant to the last damage type it took (until next round).
3. **Errata:** A random 10-foot square becomes difficult terrain (ink thickens).
4. **Margin Note:** A faint whisper reveals a clue about the escape gate (see below).

### “Bad incentive” (PvP dial)

Voltaire announces a prize:

> “Whoever ends this bout wearing the Crown of Ink gets the first key.”

At the end of Round 1, the **Crown of Ink** appears on the head of the creature who dealt the most damage that round.

- The crowned creature gets **+1 Favor immediately**.
- The crown can be stolen by reducing the crowned creature to 0 HP (nonlethal allowed) or by a contested Sleight of Hand vs Perception (as an action).

If the party cooperates and “passes” the crown intentionally, Voltaire pretends to be disappointed… and grants **+1 Favor** anyway for “interesting play”.

### Reward

After the bout, the Quillmaster “pays” with either:
- a key-tag that opens **one locker** (returning a confiscated item), or
- a “Torn Page” (single-use spell effect).

# Scene 3 — Intermission: Cages, Deals, and the Gate of Forms

Between bouts, the party gets 15 minutes (in-fiction) before the next call to the sand.

They’re hauled back into the cages. Give them:

- 1 **Ludus action** (from Scene 1), and
- a quick sponsor/rival beat (one offer, one threat, one clue).

They can attempt to open **The Gate of Forms**, a hidden exit from the ludus into the “spine tunnels” that lead out of the mindspace.

### The Gate of Forms

It has three insets: **circle**, **square**, **triangle**.

To open it, they must satisfy all three. Each has **multiple solution paths**.

#### Circle (Void / Loss)
Pick one:
- Sacrifice a **memory**: a PC chooses a personal detail; it fades (Shar approves).
- Sacrifice a **magic**: the party gives up 1 magic item until the end of the one-shot.
- Sacrifice **Favor**: spend 2 Favor as the “crowd forgets you for a moment”.

#### Square (Thought / Rule)
Pick one:
- Solve the logic: the gate asks, *“What is the rule that can be broken only by being followed?”*
  - Accept any clever answer that matches the tone (e.g., “consent”, “a promise”, “the narrative”).
- “Rules-lawyer” the lock: Persuasion/Deception DC 15 with a convincing argument.
- Force it: Thieves’ Tools DC 18 (but on failure, a guard arrives).

#### Triangle (Intention / Blood)
Pick one:
- A PC declares a vow (“I will escape,” “I will win,” etc.) and takes 1d4 piercing damage to ink the vow.
- A sponsor contract (Glasya): sign for an “exit clause” (you decide the price).
- A duel: defeat a rival gladiator in a quick, cinematic 1-round clash (nonlethal allowed).

### Clues (Three Clue Rule)

Before they attempt the gate, seed these in three different ways:
- **Graffiti** in the cell: “CIRCLE TAKES. SQUARE KNOWS. TRIANGLE WANTS.”
- **Raven drop**: three feathers (round, cut, pointed) fall in that order.
- **Voltaire whisper** during Bout 1: “Void, thought, intention—try not to mix them up.”

If they open the gate now, they gain a huge advantage in the finale (they can bypass a later hazard).
If they don’t, they can still escape after Bout 2.

# Scene 4 — Bout 2: The Protagonist Draft (PvP optional, environment mandatory)

Voltaire (standing now, hand on the crab-book like a holy text):
> “Excellent. Now: the draft. One protagonist. Or a clever loophole. Surprise me.”

### Encounter (baseline, phase 1)

- 1× **Ink-Stained Horror** (use *Hook Horror* stats, but describe it as a dripping, stitched ink-beast)

### Voltaire’s “Adjustment” (choose one)

Pick a twist that spotlights player creativity rather than shutting them down:

1. **Disadvantage Window:** For 2 rounds, attacks against it have disadvantage — *but* anyone who quotes a rule aloud gets to roll normally.
2. **Gravity Optional:** Each round, one PC can choose to ignore difficult terrain and jump absurdly far — *but* must land on a “word” (a marked tile).
3. **Errata Spawn:** When blood hits the sand, it spawns 1 HP “errata letters” (harmless, but block movement).

### Phase 2: The Glyph appears (and the rules get personal)

When the Ink-Stained Horror drops below half HP (or at the end of Round 3), the center of the arena lights up with a glyph: **Narrative Importance**.

Voltaire:
> “Behold. The reason you exist.”

Then add:
- 2× **Illusory Doppelgangers** per PC (AC 12, 1 HP, +2 to hit, 1 psychic damage).
- Each doppelganger attacks *socially* as much as physically: accusations, secrets, “I’m the real one.”

### Reward

If the party plays to the spectacle (Favor ≥ 3), a sponsor offers:
- **Glasya clause**: a contract that guarantees escape… with a hook.
- **Shar clause**: a memory-tattoo boon (advantage on one save in the finale).

# Scene 5 — Finale: Escape, Protagonist, or “Wake Up”

### Setup

If you ran Scene 4 Phase 2, you’re already here. Otherwise, drop the glyph and doppelgangers now.

### Win condition (pick one based on your table)

**A) Cooperative (team chooses unity):**
- The glyph activates only if **three PCs** touch it together (circle/square/triangle—any three).
- If the party opened the Gate of Forms earlier, touching the glyph also **opens an escape seam** into the spine tunnels.

**B) Competitive (team chooses betrayal):**
- First PC to touch the glyph becomes “Protagonist” and ends the trial immediately.
- Voltaire rewards them with a private “editor’s note” (a secret boon or a secret cost).

### If they opened the Gate of Forms earlier

They can use the spine tunnels to:
- enter the arena from below, or
- bypass one wave of doppelgangers, or
- sabotage the glyph’s defenses.

### Conclusion

When the glyph condition is met, the arena “page” flips.

They awaken back in the temple from `Adventures/2025-11-22.md`, with the same stairs, the same air… and the same question: **did anything actually happen?**

- The “winner” (or the trio) retains a faint glowing hand-mark for 24 hours: **advantage on one saving throw**.
- Someone has a missing memory (if Circle was paid) and only notices later, when it *should* matter.
- The DM chooses one physical “proof” to keep the ambiguity sharp:
  - a torn parchment scrap in a pocket (a “Torn Page”),
  - a fresh quill-scar on the stone,
  - the dark mythallar now bearing a tiny inscription: *Machinations & Actions*.

# Scaling

If the party is lower level (≤7):
- Use *Shadow*/*Specter* as written, but reduce counts.
- Replace the Hook Horror with a *Gibbering Mouther* (ink-and-mouths) or a *Gargoyle* (paper statue).

If higher level (≥13):
- Add 1–2 arena hazards (ink flood, falling “book spines”, silence zones).
- Give the Ink-Stained Horror legendary actions (1/round: move, swipe, or ink-blind).
