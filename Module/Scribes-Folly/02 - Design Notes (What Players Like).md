# Design Notes (What Players Like)

## “Arena one-shots” that land well

Common failure mode: the arena is the *only* thing that exists, so it becomes a flat sequence of fights.

The fix: make the *ludus* (prison/training barracks) and the *patrons* matter.

- **Downtime pressure**: short windows between bouts for planning, bribery, sabotage, recovery.
- **Rivals & allies**: at least 1 “friend”, 1 “enemy”, 1 “wildcard” among the other gladiators.
- **Audience favor**: a simple meter players can influence (roleplay *and* tactics) and then spend for help.
- **Clear win condition**: “escape”, “earn freedom”, or “break the arena” — not “fight forever”.

## What people tend to love in “standard” modules with adjacent vibes

These are patterns worth stealing (without copying content):

- **A strong, present villain**: felt constantly (voice, signs, consequences), rarely “on screen” until the end.
- **A consistent, punchy tone**: horror stays horror; weird stays weird; comedy is a release valve, not a reset.
- **Player agency inside constraints**: even if the hook is forced, the *path to victory* has multiple routes.
- **Memorable set pieces**: 2–3 scenes everyone will retell later (the “arena bout with a twist”, etc.).
- **Avoid NPC overload**: too many tag-alongs becomes DM homework instead of player play.

## Puzzle reliability (don’t let it stall)

If a clue is required to progress, assume it can be missed. Build redundancy:

- Give **at least 3 different clues** that point to the same conclusion (different sources, different skills).
- Ensure there’s always a “brute force” or “pay a cost” fallback (time, resources, audience favor, etc.).

## “Unwinnable” fights (how to make them feel fair)

If the opening fight is unwinnable, the win condition must change:

- Say it out loud (in-world is fine): “You can’t win. You can only choose what you carry forward.”
- Put it on a **short clock** (3–5 rounds) so it stays epic, not exhausting.
- Let success earn concrete advantages: extra gear kept, Favor, an ally/rival, a shortcut, a clue.
- Avoid “gotcha deaths”: reduce PCs to 0 if you want the drama, then capture/transition immediately.

## PvP pressure (how to keep it fun)

“Players can fight each other” is only fun if it stays optional and reversible:

- Use **incentives** (keys, Favor, sponsor boons), not hard compulsion.
- Make **nonlethal** a default assumption inside the arena unless the group explicitly wants lethal PvP.
- If the table is conflict-averse, shift rivalry into social/puzzle space and keep combat co-op.
